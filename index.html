The State of Stockpiling: What's Up with Prices for Early COVID Sellout Items?

By Hannah Bottum

With the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic in March 2020, people in the U.S. started stockpiling certain items. Toilet paper, paper towels, and cleaning supplies were among the items people were stockpiling. What else? Hair dye. Flour, to feed the sourdough craze.

Demand for these items has spiked periodically wit surges, and supply is further complicated by ongoing supply chain disruptions due to COVID-19 and other geopolitical challenges.

So what's the state of pricing for these early sellout items now?
